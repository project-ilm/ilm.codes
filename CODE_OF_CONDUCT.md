# Code of Conduct

**Respect all. Stand for your own.**

- Be respectful and professional. Disagreement is welcome — with evidence, not hostility.
- Keep it academic and technical. No political, religious, or communal advocacy; no harassment.
- Respect national narratives and the dignity of all participants, including those from nations in conflict.
- Respect data sovereignty: do not pressure anyone to share primary or restricted community materials.
- Attack ideas, never people. Bring validators *and* invalidators; let the work speak.

Violations may be addressed by warning, edit, removal of content, or removal of access, at the maintainers'
discretion, exercised conservatively and in writing. Report concerns via a private issue or repository contact.
