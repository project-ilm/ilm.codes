Public Record & RTI Strategy — Hindawi / Romenagri / ILM

A documented public record of institutional awareness and non-engagement, 2004–2026. Filed under the Right to Information Act 2005 for public interest.

What this is

The Hindawi Programming System (HPS) and Romenagri transliteration framework represent a 22-year prior art record in reversible multiscript computing infrastructure. Government bodies documented awareness through awards and recognitions from 2004–2008. Subsequent government language-technology programmes (Bhashini ₹495.51 crore; NLTM ₹73.97 crore; VYOMA) proceeded without engaging, citing, or building on this prior art. This page documents that record and the RTI filings seeking confirmation of what internal records show.

RTI Filings — Status

Filed / to be filed under the Right to Information Act 2005. The RTI chain is designed so that each response either documents the record or, by its absence, constitutes a finding. No response can bluff the record: the records either exist or their absence is the finding. Escalation ladder: 30 days → First Appeal S.19(1) → CIC S.19(3) → S.20 penalty exposure.

Why public RTI?

This is not litigation. It is the use of a statutory transparency mechanism to convert an inferential record into a documentary one. The government's own agencies (C-DAC, TDIL) and the highest constitutional office documented awareness of and conferred recognition on this work between 2004–2008. The subsequent two decades of government language-technology programmes proceeded without engaging it. RTI converts that inference into a verifiable, appeal-able, penalty-enforced public record.
