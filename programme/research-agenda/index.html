A Global Research Agenda for Linguistics Departments

This document invites linguistics departments worldwide to contribute their core scientific expertise — the study of human language — within a federated, protocol-compliant framework, through fourteen independently pursuable research work packages spanning language characterization, dialect and phonological documentation, morphology, syntax, semantics, pragmatics, terminology, lexicography, script and transliteration research, knowledge representation, corpus construction, and validation. Each institution retains ownership of its data and research; interoperability emerges through shared protocols, not centralization.
