Pillar I — Planetary Preservation at 2×2 km Resolution

This document presents Pillar I as a planetary programme to preserve human linguistic and cultural heritage at the finest practical spatial resolution, using a two-by-two-kilometre field unit as the canonical research cell, with a parametric per-unit cost model and a worked planetary estimate. The figures (on the order of US$3.25 trillion in 2026 terms, and approximately US$6.8 trillion nominal at completion over thirty years) are explicit planning estimates under stated assumptions, presented as an upper bound that the federated, amortized model is expressly designed not to incur.
