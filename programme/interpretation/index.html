Common Interpretive Frameworks — How to Read ILM

This document addresses the common interpretive frameworks that lead readers to misclassify ILM as merely a localization effort or a new programming language, rather than the civilization-scale computational-identity architecture it is. It catalogues six narrowing lenses (localization, programming-language, application-layer, academic-credential, history-linearity, and reductionist), names what each overlooks, and prescribes how to engage accurately: evaluate across layers, treat computational identity as the central invariant, study the full stack from RTL to AGI, recognise innovation outside the dominant canon, and judge by architecture, rigour, and empirical outcomes.
