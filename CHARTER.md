# The ILM Charter

Project ILM is an open, academic, civilizational research program that must work across every border —
including between people and nations who disagree. **Respect all. Stand for your own.**
*Linguistic diversity is not a bug; it is the memory of humanity. वसुधैव कुटुम्बकम्.*

1. **Scope — academic & technical only.** Scripts, languages, orthography, programming, the computing stack.
   Not a venue for political, religious, or communal advocacy.
2. **Respect across geopolitics.** Anyone from any nation may participate, including unfriendly nations. We
   respect national narratives and do not adjudicate them. The canonical layer is narrative-neutral: it
   records orthographic/linguistic identity, not political claims.
3. **Sovereignty of primary data.** We do not host or redistribute primary sociolinguistic field data,
   restricted/sacred texts, or unpublished corpora. Contribute derived, published, citable artifacts.
4. **Verifiability.** Back claims with a verifiable **DOI** (Zenodo/OSF/journal). Bring your own validators
   and invalidators. Let the work speak.
5. **Publish first, then PR.** For substantive contributions, publish and/or file (and timestamp) before the
   pull request, to protect your authorship; the PR references the DOI'd artifact.
6. **Languages beyond MT.** Low-resource languages are first-class. Provide a widely-supported pivot language
   for review; keep the original DOI-verifiable.
7. **Licensing.** Code GPL-3.0-or-later; docs/posters CC BY 4.0. Contributions must use a compatible license.
   You are responsible for your contribution's license and your right to contribute it.
8. **Stewardship.** Maintainers may decline, or later remove, anything that conflicts with this charter or the
   project's vision, mission, and objectives. Exercised conservatively and in writing.
9. **Vision/Mission/Objectives.** Vision: linguistic equity as a foundation for humane AGI. Mission: a
   canonical reversible identity layer beneath every script, language and standard, reusing the existing
   ecosystem. Objectives: linguistic equity, identity preservation, reversibility, ecosystem reuse, execution
   independence.
