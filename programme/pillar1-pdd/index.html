Pillar I — Cultural & Cognitive Preservation (PDD)

Pillar I specifies the federated scientific infrastructure for the preservation, formalization, and computational representation of human linguistic knowledge — every language, dialect, idiolect, and script — at fine geographic and cognitive resolution. It defines the research lifecycle (observe, document, validate, represent, formalize, publish, version, revalidate, continuously improve), the data model, the privacy-by-design federation, and the canonical computational identity into which all preserved knowledge is mapped. Pillar I preserves knowledge; it does not, by itself, compute with it.
